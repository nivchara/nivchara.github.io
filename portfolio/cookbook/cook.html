Cinnamon Toast

A cheap breakfast that is reminiscent of a classic cereal.

List of Ingredients and supplies:

  • Bread, any type will work well, including gluten free!
  • Cinnamon Sugar
  • Butter
  • Food-safe blow torch
  • Optional: Vanilla Drizzle

Steps:

  1. Toast your bread
  2. Butter bread
  3. Sprinkle cinnamon sugar on top
  4. Lightly torch the top of the cinnamon sugar until it is caramelized
  5. Enjoy!

Fried Eggs

A common staple across many cultures. Boil 'em, mash 'em, put them in a stew!

List of Ingredients and supplies:

  • Eggs
  • A Pan
  • Butter or Oil
  • Stovetop/Range
  • Seasonings

Steps:

  1. Preheat pan on stovetop
  2. Butter or oil pan
  3. Crack egg INTO preheated pan
  4. Cook egg until it is done
  5. Turn off heat
  6. Transfer egg(s) onto plate, or bowl, or mouth
  7. CONSUME

Walking Tacos

A delicious way to eat tacos, without the mess. Often found at tailgating events or football games.

List of Ingredients and Supplies

  • Bag of corn chips
  • Cooked ground beef, seasoned with taco seasoning
  • Sour Cream
  • Cheddar Cheese
  • Diced Onions
  • Shredded Lettuce

Steps

  1. Prepare your vegetables
  2. Pre-cook the meat and add seasoning when finished
  3. Open bag of corn chips
  4. Place ingredients inside the bag of corn chips
  5. Mix well
  6. Walk around with your "taco"!

BBQ Chicken

Wonderfully air fried chicken with bbq sauce, without having to heat up a grill or oven!

List of Ingredients and Supplies

  • Bone-in Skin-On Chicken thighs
  • Sweet Baby Rays Original BBQ Sauce
  • Air Fryer
  • Food safe thermometer

Steps

  1. Separate the chicken skin from the meat
  2. Rub BBQ sauce directly onto the meat and place the skin back on
  3. Set air fryer to 400 degrees and time for 20-25 minutes
  4. Place the chicken skin side up and fry for 20 minutes or until the thickest park of the chicken registers 165 degrees
  5. Once finished cooking, let sit for 10 minutes and enjoy!

Potato Packs

Though not politically correct, this "Hobo Pack" is great for campers who are short on space and want an easy, hands off meal

List of Ingredients and Supplies

  • Campfire
  • Aluminum Foil
  • Potatoes
  • Protein of choice
  • Other vegetables of choice
  • Grill tongs
  • Optional: Food safe thermometer

Steps

  1. Set up a campfire, or grill. Ideally, it should be around 300 degrees
  2. Arrange ingredients into some foil and season to taste
  3. Wrap tightly with foil and throw directly into the fire, under hot coals
  4. Cook until the potatoes are fork tender and/or until the meat (if applicable) has reached a food safe temperature.
  5. Remove from fire with tongs and set aside until hot, but not burning
  6. Remove foil and enjoy!
